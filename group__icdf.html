Detailed Description

In probability and statistics, the inverse cumulative distribution function, sometimes called a quantile function is, associated with a probability distribution of a random variable, specifies the value of the random variable such that the probability of the variable being less than or equal to that value equals the given probability.

See https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantile_function for more information.

Lognormal Inverse CDF

\begin{aligned} F^{-1}(p) &= \exp(\mu + \sqrt{2} \, \sigma \, \text{inv_erf}(2p - 1)) \\ &= \exp(\mu + \sigma \, \Phi^{âˆ’1}(x)) \end{aligned}

because

\begin{aligned} \Phi(x) &= \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^x e^{-t^2}dt \\ &= \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\text{erf}\bigg( \frac{x}{\sqrt{2}} \bigg) \end{aligned}

implies that \( \Phi^{-1}(x) = \sqrt{2} \, \text{inv_erf}(2x - 1) \).
